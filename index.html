Scene 2 — Problem & Context

Why Disinformation Is a Youth Crisis

Young Europeans aged 15–24 are the most prolific digital users — yet many lack the skills to critically evaluate what they see, share, and believe. The consequences reach far beyond individual decisions.

📱
67%
of EU youth encounter disinformation weekly on social media
Click for source →
🔎
42%
cannot distinguish credible news from manipulated content
Click for source →
🗳️
58%
say disinformation has influenced their views on political topics
Click for source →

This is not a technology problem — it is an education and resilience problem. Schools, youth organisations, and municipalities are not equipped with the tools and training to address it systematically. The SpeakUp project is designed to close that gap.

Scene 3 — Objectives & Target Groups

What SpeakUp Commits To

Four specific objectives, delivered through a structured transnational partnership of youth NGOs, schools, municipalities, and digital content experts.

🛡️

Critical Digital Literacy Training

Train 500 young people in verifying online content, identifying manipulation tactics, and applying fact-checking methodologies

🧑‍🏫

Youth Worker Capacity Building

Equip 80 youth workers and educators across 6 countries with peer-to-peer facilitation skills and curriculum tools

🌐

Open Educational Resource Hub

Produce a multilingual, freely licensed library of 30+ training modules, lesson plans, and facilitation guides

📣

Policy Dialogue & Advocacy

Organise 4 policy roundtables with local authorities and submit 2 evidence-based policy briefs to national governments

Scene 4 — Activities & Work Packages

How We Deliver It

Five structured work packages over 24 months. Click any package to see details, timeline, and lead organisation.

1

WP1 — Coordination & Quality Management

Governance framework, financial oversight, inter-partner communication, risk management, and reporting to EACEA

M1–M24 Lead: Project Factory EU Output: Management reports, risk register
2

WP2 — Needs Assessment & Research

Baseline surveys across 6 countries on disinformation exposure, digital literacy gaps, and existing educational frameworks

M1–M5 Lead: University of Gdańsk Output: 6 country reports + synthesis
3

WP3 — Curriculum & Resource Development

Co-creation of 30+ training modules, fact-checking toolkits, and educator guides in 6 languages. Youth-tested and peer-reviewed

M4–M10 Lead: SpeakUp NGO (Germany) Output: Open OER library
4

WP4 — Training & Peer Learning Delivery

500 youth trained across 6 countries. 80 youth workers trained as facilitators. 3 transnational youth exchanges included

M8–M20 Lead: All partners Output: Training reports, participant records
5

WP5 — Dissemination, Policy & Evaluation

Final conference in Brussels, independent external evaluation, 2 policy briefs, and multimedia dissemination campaign

M18–M24 Lead: Project Factory EU Output: Final report, policy briefs, conference
